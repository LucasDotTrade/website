The problem

PDFs are the system of record, but they are not machine-readable.

Trade teams spend time re-keying fields, reconciling inconsistencies, and chasing missing pages. Risk shows up as small mismatches across documents, and it is easy to miss without a standardized file format.

Before

Manual intake, unclear packets

Mixed scans, duplicates, and version confusion. Teams reconstruct the packet before they can even start review.

During

Re-keying, reconciliation, exceptions

Small inconsistencies across LC terms, invoice, and transport docs can create delays and disputes.

After

Audit pressure, limited traceability

When questions come later, it is hard to prove what was reviewed, what was flagged, and what was decided.

How it works

Standardize the packet, then review the exceptions.

LUCAS.trade creates a consistent trade file from messy inputs, extracts key fields with traceability to source pages, highlights mismatches for reviewer attention, then exports an audit-ready pack plus structured data.
